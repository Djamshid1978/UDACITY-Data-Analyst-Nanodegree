Data analysis: Project Titanic dataset

Dataset

In 1912, the ship RMS Titanic struck an iceberg on its maiden voyage and sank, resulting in the deaths of most of its passengers and crew. The data contains demographics and voyage information from 891 of the 2224 passengers and crew on board the ship. This link allows reading the description of this dataset on the Kaggle website, where the data was obtained. https://www.kaggle.com.

In this project, the most interesting variable I could observe here is "Survived". The main question is: What factors made people more likely to survive?

We are going to review the factors whether the number of survivors was dependent on their age, sex, social status (class and fare).

In this purpose first we can analysis material status of passengers, having considered fares by classes.

Then we can analysis how many people survived having considered them by class and by sex.

Also we can analysis Age of survived passengers, having considered minimum and maximum age of survived passengers, how many children survived and so on.

First of all, we have to exract data from the file (titanic-data) and look at the available indicators.

Conclusions Phase.

Looking at the above plots, it seems to me that females had more chances to survive than males, as well as the passengers of the first class had more chances than the passengers of the second class, and they had more chances than passengers of third class.

There are a lot of limitations in this analysis.

We have the data for only 891 passengers, but in this incident there were still the rest 1333 passengers in total and the crew members. If the rest of the part of survived passengers will be more in 2nd or 3rd class, the result of all the analysis should be completely different.

Some columns have missing data and it also could affect the analysis. For example, the trends in age categories and in the distribution of them of rescued passengers may be changed significantly.

Through the analysis of this sample dataset I found out that sum of surviving passengers was 342 out of the 891 people, which consist about 38%. I would say survivors of passengers is much higher among women than among men, because I guess it was humanitarian factor when people rescue women, children and elders at first. Responding to my above mentioned main question: What factors made people more likely to survive? I do not want to say that the preference was given only for the passengers of the first class. Maybe it was so, but if we look at number of passengers by class and survived passengers by class, we can see that there is almost proportionality in each class depending on the number Pclass. I think that was a very good index for every number of classes in this panic situation.
